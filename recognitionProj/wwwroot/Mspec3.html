Understanding Academic Ranks

Professor: The highest academic rank, requiring a Ph.D., with extensive research, teaching, and publications. Full professors may or may not be tenured.

Associate Professor: A mid-level rank, requiring a Ph.D., with notable research and teaching experience. Tenure may or may not be granted at this level.

Assistant Professor: An entry-level faculty position, requiring a Ph.D., focused on teaching and research. This position is not tenured.

Professor of Practice: Typically requires at least a bachelor’s degree and a minimum of 10 years of significant industry experience. This rank is applicable only to practical or applied programs , Professors of Practice may focus more on bridging industry practices with academic instruction rather than pursuing traditional research or tenure.

Lecturer with Ph.D.: Requires a Ph.D., with a deep focus on both teaching and research. This position is not tenured.

Lecturer with MSc: Requires an MSc degree, with a focus on both teaching and research. This is a junior academic position, and tenure is usually granted.

Assistant Lecturer: A teaching-focused position, typically requiring an MSc degree. This position is not tenured and often held before obtaining a Ph.D.
